Step-by-Step Rebuild Guide

Tools & Parts Needed

● King rebuild kit (seals, o-rings, shock oil).

● Shock vise and seal bullet tool.

● Nitrogen fill tool.

● Gloves, goggles, safety equipment.

Step 1: Safely Depressurize

Release nitrogen pressure before opening the shock.

Step 2: Disassembly

Remove bypass tubes, shafts, and internal seals.

Step 3: Cleaning

Clean with solvent, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage seals.

Step 4: Seal Replacement

Install new seals and o-rings, lubricated lightly.

Step 5: Refill with Oil

Use recommended King shock oil; bleed out air bubbles.Trapped bubbles can cause cavitation and inconsistent damping, reducing overall performance.

Step 6: Recharge with Nitrogen

Fill to proper PSI (varies by vehicle/tuning setup).

Tuning for Maximum Control

External Adjustments

● Compression adjusters: Control how quickly shocks absorb impacts.

● Rebound adjusters: Control how fast suspension resets after compression.

Internal Tuning (Advanced)

● Shim stacks and piston valving allow deeper customization.

● Recommended only for experienced tuners or professional shops.

Tuning by Application

● Desert Racing - Stiffer compression, faster rebound.

● Overlanding - Softer compression for comfort, controlled rebound.

● Rock Crawling - Slower rebound for maximum grip.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

● Over-pressurizing nitrogen.

● Using incorrect oil viscosity.

● Skipping inspection of shafts and piston.

● Forgetting bypass tube alignment.

FAQ (Buyer-Focused)

Q: How often should these shocks be rebuilt?
A: For racing, after every major event. For recreational use, every 10,000–15,000 miles. Regular rebuilds prevent internal wear, extend service life, and ensure consistent damping when conditions get rough.

Q: Can they be rebuilt at home?
A: Yes, with the proper tools and a rebuild kit. Many owners choose DIY to save costs. However, professional servicing is recommended if you don’t have access to nitrogen-charging equipment or a clean workspace.

Q: What nitrogen PSI should I use?
A: It varies by vehicle weight and application, but typically ranges from 150–250 PSI. Lighter vehicles may require lower pressure, while heavier rigs or racing setups need the higher end for optimal performance.

Q: How do they compare to coilovers?
A: This type of shock offers far more adjustability for compression and rebound, making it superior for heavy off-road use. Coilovers work well for moderate setups, but bypass designs allow fine-tuning for specific terrain and driving styles.

Q: How long does a rebuild usually take?
A: With the right tools, most experienced owners can complete the process in a few hours. Beginners may take longer, but step-by-step rebuild kits and guides make the process much more approachable.
